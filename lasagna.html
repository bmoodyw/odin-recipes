Lasagna

Description

Homemade lasagna is the best. It tastes nothing like the ones from the frozen food aisle. This recipe is so good—it’s the kind of lasagna people write home about! It brings together all of the things we love in a good pasta dish: noodles, cheeses, fresh herbs and a delicious meat sauce. If you’ve never made lasagna before or haven’t found a good recipe, you have come to the right place. Invite a friend because you won’t want to keep this dish to yourself!

Pasta Shortcut

Boiling lasagna noodles is an extra step that we would all love to avoid. No boil lasagna noodles, in our opinion, have an unpleasant texture and often turn out soggy. A hack to skip boiling is to place regular lasagna noodles in a 9×13 pan and pour hot water over them. Let them soak for 30 minutes, and they’ll be tender enough to use in the recipe. Don’t worry, they will finish cooking in the oven.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound sweet Italian sausage
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 large white onion diced
  • 5 cloves of garlic minced
  • 28 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 15 oz can tomatoe sauce

Steps

  1. In a large pot over medium heat, add in ground sausage and ground beef. Use a spoon to break up the meat into small pieces
  2. Add in onion and garlic and cook until meat is well browned, stirring constantly.
  3. Stir in sugar, fresh basil, fennel, oregano, 1/2 teaspoon salt, pepper, and 1/4 cup chopped parsley.
  4. Pour in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce, and wine*. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  5. Reduce heat to low and simmer 1-4 hours, stirring occasionally.
